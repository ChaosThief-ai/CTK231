About This Site (h2)

M2 didn’t work quite right. It wasn’t as successful as M1 in terms of replicating personality, and Edwin wasn’t sure why. Instead of his son returning to him, what he had instead was the haphazard recreation of him, further reminding Edwin of his son’s death in its presence. While M2 was designed to mimic David, it also began to copy other things. Eventually, Edwin had enough of this shattered version of his son. Edwin’s mental state had been declining for years at this point as he got less work from other companies other than Fredbear’s. In an outburst of rage and guilt over what had happened to his son, the loss of his wife, and his failing business, he fought the robot, attacking it with all his might, smashing the metal and trying to tear pieces off. Around this time, the head of the business side of Fredbear’s, William Afton, was offering better jobs to those working at Edwin’s failing business, poaching employees from him. Edwin was so desperate for more jobs that he failed to notice the fine print on one of the latest contracts from Fredbear’s. According to the contract, if Edwin was unable to fulfil his obligations, all his property, assets, and land would belong to Fredbear’s, and with William siphoning Edwin of his employees, and changing demands at the last minute, Edwin’s downfall would be an inevitability.

Eventually, updates on the project from Edwin stopped, and Fredbear’s now owned the entire workshop as well as Edwin’s house. When a team was sent to look around, they saw M2, and realized its potential. Unfortunately for them, this Mimic endoskeleton had learned to mimic violence from Edwin’s outburst, and attacked the team. Fredbear’s Family Diner heard about this, and they wanted their hands on the tech. Having a robotic skeleton that could be taught through visuals and through programming would be a huge asset to the company, especially since they had begun to branch out into robotic entertainers in the late 70s.

More teams were sent to the workshop, all of them except for one surviving. Fredbear’s—soon to be Freddy Fazbear’s—had acquired the Mimic technology. There is a pretty big time gap here. We pick back up in the early 80s, specifically 1983. William’s youngest son Dave (not David!) has his birthday party at Fredbear’s. The thing is, he hates it there! Something that he saw had him freaked out about the robots on stage, and his teenage brother Mike wouldn’t stop mocking him for it.
