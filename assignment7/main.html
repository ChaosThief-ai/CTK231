Ok, since this is meant to be filler text and I’m done with the assignment a few days early, I’ll just recap the entire Five Nights at Freddy’s timeline for you. Know that these plot beats are not 100% confirmed and are just my interpretation of the story. Five Nights at Freddy’s is notoriously sparse on the details, especially in terms of character motivations and concrete dates, but that’s what I think makes theorizing about it so fun! Anyways, here is my interpretation of the full story and timeline. It’s very long, so I’m not sure these sites will hold everything, but we’ll see!

Another h2

Our story begins in the late 60s, where inventor Edwin Murray Sr. passes away, leaving his workshop to his adult son, Edwin Murray Jr, who will simply be named ‘Edwin’ from here on out. Edwin was much less put-together than his father in terms of organization but showed a lot of promise in his inventions. His first invention since inheriting the workshop, the Clean-O-Vac, was a massive hit! He and his wife Fiona were very happy together now that the company was successful under his leadership. Edwin continued to invent and tinker, but none of his other inventions seemed to take off like the Clean-O-Vac did. One day, he was approached by someone from the local Fredbear’s Family Diner, asking for his help in creating mascots and costumes for the local pizzeria. Edwin agreed, and after signing the contract, he got to work on helping Fredbear’s Family Diner. The restaurant was branching out a bit, and was a major player in the town’s local Fall Fest, entertaining the masses with their mascot characters. With Edwin’s help, they’d be able to create carnival games and robotic attractions! Everything was great for the two businesses, until disaster struck. Fiona had left to check on the Fall Fest crew, who was setting up for the event, when a fire broke out, leading to her untimely death. This left Edwin without a wife, and his young son without a mother. Edwin turned this loss into motivation to stray from his contract, and he created a new robot he called M1. He used training data, stories, and personal history from his wife to program the robot into acting and sounding exactly like Fiona. Surprisingly, this worked! Edwin had his wife again, as if she never died. He kept on fulfilling his contract for Fredbear’s, leaving babysitting duties to M1. Edwin’s son David was afraid of M1 at first, but quickly grew attached to it since he now had a playmate. Edwin was very neglectful of his son because of how busy he was, and now that David had someone to pass the time with, he was much happier.

Unfortunately, this happiness didn’t last. When David was left unattended with M1, he was tragically killed. Whether it was from a car, heavy machinery, or otherwise, it didn’t matter. Edwin had first lost his wife, and now had lost his son. M1 would press him shortly after, asking him to rebuild David, like how he had rebuilt her. Edwin denied her over and over again, but eventually, he relented. He transferred the programming of M1 into a machine he developed called F10N4, leaving the metal skeleton without programming. He then did the same thing he did with his late wife, attempting to program his son’s consciousness into the robot. When it was done, he dubbed the robot “M2”.
